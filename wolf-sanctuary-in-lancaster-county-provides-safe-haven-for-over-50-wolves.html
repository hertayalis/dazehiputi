Jaw-Dropping Wolf Sanctuary: 50+ Wolves Find Epic Safe Haven!

The Wolf Sanctuary of PA, situated in Lititz, Lancaster County, is renowned as one of the largest wolf sanctuaries in the nation. It provides a home to 56 gray wolves and wolf-dogs that have been rescued from various conditions, including abuse cases, private pet owners, and zoos.

The primary objective is to offer these majestic creatures a lifelong haven within the 80-acre sanctuary. The dedicated staff, including numerous volunteers, attends to the wolves’ every requirement, ensuring they have nutritious diets, comfortable habitats, and thoughtful social interactions with their fellow wolves.

The sanctuary offers group tours on designated days, as well as private tours that can be scheduled. These tours provide visitors with the opportunity to observe these exquisite animals up close. Information about tour days and times can be accessed through the sanctuary’s website.

Operating as a nonprofit, the sanctuary heavily relies on guided tours and contributions from its supporters. Officials emphasize that their 40-year existence is owed to the community’s generous backing.

At present, efforts are underway to gather funds for the renovation of a stallion pen that has stood on the property since the late 1700s. The aim is to transform this structure into an onsite veterinary suite, catering to the comprehensive needs of all the animals.
