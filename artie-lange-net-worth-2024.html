Artie Lange’s Early Life and Career

Arthur Steven Lange Jr., known professionally as Artie Lange, was born in Livingston, New Jersey. He began his career in the entertainment industry as a stand-up comedian, performing in various comedy clubs in New York City. His big break came when he joined the cast of the popular sketch comedy show, “Mad TV,” in 1995.

Transition to Radio and Television

After leaving “Mad TV,” Lange joined the Howard Stern Show, where he quickly became a fan favorite. His tenure on the show significantly increased his popularity and contributed to his growing wealth. In addition to his radio work, Lange also appeared in several films and television shows, further diversifying his income sources.
