Meet VTV Ganeshs Parents R. B. Choudary And Mahjabeen

VTV Ganesh is an Indian actor, comedian, and film producer. He is best known for his hoarse voice and appearances in films starring Silambarasan. He made his film debut in Singampuri’s 2002 Tamil film Red. He is best known for the film Vinnaithaandi Varuvaayaa (2010), directed by Gautam Menon.

Prior to VTV, he had minor roles in films such as Red (2002), Vettaiyaadu Vilaiyaadu (2006), Pachaikili Muthucharam (2007), and Vaaranam Aayiram (2008).

Ganesh broke through with his role in Vinaithandi Varvaya (2010), his own production directed by Gautam Vasudev Menon and starring Silambarasan and Trisha Krishnan. His portrayal of Ganesh, the fictional cinematographer of Vinaithaandi Varuvaiya (2010), was critically acclaimed, eventually renaming him by adding the film’s initials (VTV) as a prefix.

He had a comical role in the anthology film Vaanam (2011) which he produced, portraying the character of ‘Bhajanai’ Ganesh who assisted Cable Raja when he played Silambarathan. He also starred, with Silambarasan playing the role of the heroine’s father.

In 2012, Ganesh played the role of Silambaran’s uncle in Vignesh Sivan’s romantic musical film Podha Podi, for which he was nominated for the Vijay Award and the SIIMA Award for Best Comedian. He was one of the main characters in Naveena Saraswathi Sabatham (2013).

In 2014, VTV Ganesh starred in, wrote, and produced Vincent Selva’s comedy Inga Enna Solluthu. He later appeared in several more, including Kappal (2014), Romeo Juliet (2015), Vaalu (2015), Vaaliba Raja (2016), Motta Shiva Ketta Shiva (2017), Sakka Podu Podu Raja (2017). He also appeared in Vijay’s Beast (2022) and Varisu (2023).
